# Professional Philosophy

## Controls Should Survive Contact With Reality

A control that only works in the framework document isn't a control — it's a hope. Across my roles at Grab, the work that's held up over time has been the work built with the people who'd actually run it day to day: analysts doing the casework, engineers shipping the platform, Policy setting the regulatory boundary. Designing in isolation from any of those groups tends to produce controls that look good and don't survive first contact with real case volume.

## Investigation Efficiency and Investigation Quality Are Not Opposites

Reducing per-case handling time (from 30 minutes to 10, for example) only matters if evidence accuracy and decision consistency go up alongside it — not just throughput. The automation and AI-assisted tooling I've worked on has been built with that pairing in mind: faster case handling that also standardizes decisioning, rather than faster case handling that trades away rigor.

## Root Cause Over Recurring Firefighting

Individual case resolution is necessary but not sufficient. Every investigation is also a data point about whether an upstream control is doing its job. Root cause analysis — and feeding it back into control design, SOPs, and training — is where fraud operations work compounds instead of just repeating.

## Regional Consistency Without Regional Uniformity

Operating across five Southeast Asian markets means the same control can't be deployed identically everywhere and expected to work the same way. Consistency in outcomes and consistency in mechanism are different goals, and conflating them is a common way regional programs quietly under-perform in the markets furthest from where the framework was designed.

## Law Enforcement and Regulatory Partnership Is Relationship Work

Being the primary liaison to the Singapore Police Force isn't a process step — it's a relationship that has to be maintained with responsiveness, clear documentation, and follow-through, case after case. The trust built there is what makes escalation and scam-tracing work actually functional in urgent situations.
