**Purpose:** Frame this portfolio in one page.
**Estimated reading time:** 3 minutes

## What this is

A documented set of fraud investigations, each modeled on real casework from 7+ years in fraud operations
and trust & safety at a Southeast Asian digital payments company, restaged against a fictional company
(**NovaPay**) to protect confidentiality.

## What this demonstrates

- Structuring a fraud investigation from business problem to executive recommendation — not starting from SQL
- Translating SQL findings into risk assessments and operational recommendations
- Working across chargebacks, account takeover, merchant risk, and cross-market fraud trend reporting
- Communicating findings to both operational and leadership audiences
- Law-enforcement-facing investigation discipline (evidence handling, structured findings, defensible conclusions)

## Why it's structured this way

Fraud operations work is judged on outcomes: was the right risk correctly identified, was the response
proportionate, and was the recommendation something the business could actually act on. Each investigation
in this folder is written to be defensible in exactly that way — a hiring manager or interviewer should be
able to ask "walk me through this" and get a real answer, not a recited SQL script.
